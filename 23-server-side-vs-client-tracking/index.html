Growth Engineering Playbook · Wave 4

Your analytics sees barely half of what happens.

Client-side tags lose events to ad-block, ITP and cookie expiry, and beacons that never fire — and the loss is biased by browser and device, so it quietly distorts every channel number. Server-side tracking wins most of that back, but it is not a consent workaround, and running client and server together double-counts unless you dedup on a shared event id. This models the fictional Northstar Outfitters traffic through each architecture, draws the consent ceiling no stack can beat, and separates the loss you can recover from the loss that's gone regardless. The Decision

The five traffic segments are the fictional Northstar Outfitters profile in scenarios.js (true event volume + per-cause loss rates) — invented, no real analytics data or PII. Client capture = consent × (1−ad-block)(1−ITP)(1−beacon); server capture = consent × (1−server-error); deduped hybrid is the union; naive hybrid sums the two. Part of the Growth Engineering Playbook.
