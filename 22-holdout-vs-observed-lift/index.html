The channel reported the sale. It didn't cause it.

Every platform reports the conversions it touched — but touching a sale isn't causing it. The only honest way to know what your spend actually added is a holdout: withhold the campaign from a random control group and measure the gap. This turns seven fictional Northstar Outfitters holdout tests into true incremental lift — a two-proportion z-test with confidence intervals, and reported-vs-incremental ROAS — so demand-harvesting channels stop taking credit for sales that were coming anyway. The Decision

The seven holdout tests are the fictional Northstar Outfitters mix in experiments.js (treatment/control users + conversions, spend, revenue) — invented, no real brands or PII. Incremental lift = treatment rate − control rate; significance is a two-proportion z-test; incremental ROAS values the lift at each channel's average order value.
